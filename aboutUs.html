OUR STORY

Our Heritage

FAMILY TRADITION

Founded in 2003 by visionary chef Bram Rodriguez, BramFood began as a small family bistro in Dominican Republic's historic district. Drawing from three generations of culinary expertise, we've transformed traditional Mediterranean recipes into contemporary masterpieces.

Master Chef

BRAM RODRIGUEZ

With over 25 years of culinary excellence, Chef Bram combines classical training from Le Cordon Bleu with innovative techniques. His signature fusion of Mediterranean and Asian influences has earned our restaurant three prestigious Michelin stars.

Culinary Philosophy

FARM TO TABLE

At BramFood, we believe in sourcing only the finest seasonal ingredients from local farmers. Since 2008, our commitment to sustainability has made us pioneers in the farm-to-table movement, ensuring each dish tells a story of authenticity and respect.

OUR JOURNEY

What began as a modest 10-table establishment has blossomed into one of Europe's most celebrated culinary destinations. Through Chef Bram's unwavering commitment to excellence, BramFood has expanded to five locations across Europe while maintaining the intimate, family-owned atmosphere that first captured our guests' hearts.

In 2010, we were honored with our first Michelin star, followed by a second in 2015 and a third in 2019. Our flagship Dominican Republic location underwent a complete renovation in 2017, merging rustic Mediterranean charm with contemporary design elements that reflect our culinary philosophy.

Despite international acclaim, Chef Bram remains in the kitchen daily, personally training each member of our culinary team and ensuring that every plate meets his exacting standards before reaching your table.

MEET CHEF BRAM

Born in Valencia to a family of restaurateurs, Bram Rodriguez discovered his passion for cooking at the age of seven in his grandmother's kitchen. After formal training in Paris and New York, he worked alongside culinary legends including Ferran Adrià and Thomas Keller before returning to Spain to realize his vision.

"Cooking is not just about flavor—it's about memory, emotion, and connection," says Chef Bram. "Each dish should tell a story and transport the diner to a specific moment in time."

When not in the kitchen, Chef Bram leads culinary workshops for underprivileged youth and has authored three bestselling cookbooks. His philosophy of combining technical precision with emotional resonance has influenced a generation of young chefs across Europe.
